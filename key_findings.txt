1. Extremophile RecA proteins showed modest but directional shifts in sequence-derived composition compared with controls.
2. Hydrophobic fraction showed the strongest mean difference between the two groups in this dataset.
3. PCA of the RecA feature matrix showed partial structure, with some extremophiles occupying more distinct regions while others overlapped with controls.
4. The results suggest that RecA may carry weak-to-moderate environmental signal, but not a clean universal extremophile signature.
5. Different classes of extremophiles did not collapse into one uniform cluster, suggesting heterogeneous molecular adaptation patterns.
6. A broader panel of stress-relevant proteins would likely provide stronger separation than RecA alone.
